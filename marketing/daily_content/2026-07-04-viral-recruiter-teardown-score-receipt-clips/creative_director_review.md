# Creative Director Review

Batch: `2026-07-04-viral-recruiter-teardown-score-receipt-clips`

## Swipe File Comparison

The revised batch now matches the niche pattern more closely: resume or job-description artifact appears immediately, each short focuses on one visible problem, and the score jump is backed by a receipt before the reveal. This is a significant improvement over the failed batch, which felt like a slow AI SaaS demo.

## What Is Stronger

- The videos are short enough for the format: 30.25s, 31.38s, and 31.45s.
- Each short has a distinct wrapper: red-marker roast, recruiter search console, and job-description answer key.
- The score reveal is earned by visible factors: keyword match, tool match, metric proof, and outcome clarity.
- The resumes are more professional and role-specific, with realistic headers, roles, skills, and job descriptions.
- The voice is generated through ElevenLabs with timestamp captions and a quiet music bed instead of distracting sound effects.
- Signal appears as a guide and brand marker without taking focus away from the resume/JD artifact.

## What Still Feels Weak

- The batch is review-ready, not proven viral-ready. It still needs more safe humor and more job-hunter frustration in the first two seconds.
- Two clips are near the 32-second ceiling. The next iteration should A/B test 21-26 second cuts.
- The current ElevenLabs key can synthesize with the configured voice ID, but it cannot list voices because it lacks `voices_read`. That blocks proper alternate-voice testing.
- Audio QC verifies assets, codec, duration, sample rate, and restrained volume, but it does not yet measure LUFS or true peak.
- The mascot is present and helpful, but it should get sharper micro-reactions: side-eye at vague bullets, tiny alarm on missing tools, and a small win reaction when the receipt fills.

## Post-Readiness

- `This resume sentence is quietly expensive`: closest to post-ready. The weak bullet and score receipt are logical, and the red-marker style reads fast.
- `I searched Salesforce and this resume vanished`: strongest premise. It should be the first candidate to test because the search-console concept explains the score without sounding like a product demo.
- `This resume failed an open-book test`: clear and useful, but least funny. It should pass review if the goal is credibility; it needs a punchier first caption if the goal is virality.

## Next Creative Improvements

- Add a 21-26 second render preset with fewer narration clauses.
- Add a humor line bank that punches at vague resume language, not job seekers.
- Add a voice A/B test once ElevenLabs voice listing is available.
- Add LUFS/true-peak measurement to the audio gate.
- Add one more visual family: text-message job hunter panic or courtroom evidence board.
