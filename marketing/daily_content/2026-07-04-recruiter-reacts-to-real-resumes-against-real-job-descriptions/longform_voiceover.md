# Long-form Voiceover: Recruiter reacts to real resumes against real job descriptions | Resume Teardown

This file is the narration source for the Remotion timing pass. Keep it script-only.

## Cold open

Recruiter reacts to real resumes against real job descriptions. This resume looks professional, which is why the mistake is dangerous. On screen, it feels polished. The formatting is clean, the person is probably qualified, and nothing looks obviously broken. But in a recruiter search, the proof is still hard to find. That is the whole crime scene.

## Open loop

We are not adding fake experience. We are checking whether the resume explains the real experience in the same language as the target job. If the job asks for HubSpot, CAC analysis, pipeline sourcing, and LinkedIn Ads, the resume cannot just say helped with campaigns and hope everyone uses imagination. Recruiters skim. Search terms matter. Clear proof matters more.

## Translate the job description

Before touching the resume, pull out the role title, tools, responsibilities, metrics, and proof signals the job actually asks for. For this example, the job description is basically waving a flashlight at demand generation, lifecycle campaigns, HubSpot workflows, LinkedIn Ads, MQL to SQL conversion, and pipeline impact. Those are not decorations. Those are the clues the resume needs to answer.

## Recruiter search test

Now run the recruiter search test. If someone searches for the first three important terms from the job description, this resume should produce useful hits. Right now, the source bullet is too broad. It says helped maintain HubSpot lists for webinars and nurture emails. That is real experience, but it sounds like a task list instead of a business result.

## Resume crime scene

Now compare the resume line by line. The weak bullet is not dishonest. It is just too vague to carry the role-specific proof. This is where a lot of job seekers get punished for being polite. They write safe, generic sentences because they do not want to exaggerate. Good instinct. Bad resume signal. The fix is not hype. The fix is precision.

## Why it gets missed

A polished sentence can still hide the most important evidence. Recruiters skim for tools, scope, and outcomes because those make fit easy to verify. Helped with campaigns forces the reader to guess. Managed HubSpot segmentation for webinar nurture and improved qualified handoff gives them a path. Add scope, add tool, add outcome, and the same experience becomes searchable.

## Live bullet fix

Here is the live bullet fix. Instead of helped maintain HubSpot lists for webinars and nurture emails, write: maintained HubSpot segmentation for webinar nurture programs, cleaned lead routing logic, and improved marketing qualified lead handoff for sales follow-up. If there is a real metric, use it. If there is not, do not invent one. Tools, scope, audience, and result are enough to make the bullet useful.

## Score reveal

Once the evidence matches the role language, the example score moves up. The point is not gaming a robot. The point is making real fit easier to see. Same person. Same experience. Better signal. The resume was not bad before, it was just asking the recruiter to do too much translation work in six seconds.
