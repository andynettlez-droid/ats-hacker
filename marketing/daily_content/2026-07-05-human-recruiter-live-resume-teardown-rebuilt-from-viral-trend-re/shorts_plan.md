# Shorts Plan: Human recruiter live resume teardown rebuilt from viral trend research

## Trend Research

Human premise: A recruiter-style reviewer is reading one resume line against one visible job requirement in real time.
Platform pattern: recruiter reacts / resume teardown / search test
Copy from research: Open on the resume or job post immediately; use first-person reviewer language; show one weak line, one visible source of proof, and one honest rewrite.
Avoid: AI SaaS demo pacing, generic ATS fear, old marketing-tool defaults, rubric-first narration, and score jumps that appear before the viewer sees why.

## Short 1: I would circle this line first

Series: Live Resume Review
Hook: I would circle this line first.

Script:
Okay, this line says: Created reports for business teams and leadership. I would circle it because the proof is lower on the page: Built a Tableau churn tracker that saved six hours a week and flagged 18 at-risk customer accounts. The job wants SQL, Tableau, and cohort analysis, so I would score it around 32. Rewrite it as: built a SQL churn dashboard that saved six hours a week and flagged 18 at-risk accounts. Now I can see why it scores 89. Run the free Signal score before you apply.

Storyboard:
- Case: Data Analyst Resume against Product Data Analyst.
- Open on the resume as if a reviewer is reading it at a desk.
- Circle the exact weak line: Created reports for business teams and leadership.
- Place the job description beside it with SQL, Tableau, and cohort analysis highlighted.
- Show the Signal Fit Score rubric before the score reveal.
- Rewrite the line in-place using only the real work.
- Reveal the score after the low-score rationale is visible.

## Short 2: I searched the resume. Bad news.

Series: Recruiter Search Test
Hook: I searched the resume. Bad news.

Script:
Here is the search test. I search Salesforce and pipeline generation. Then I read: Responsible for sales opportunities in assigned territory. That line makes me guess. Lower on the page, I found the proof: Generated $1.8M in qualified Salesforce pipeline through 42 monthly discovery calls and MEDDICC account notes. So I would probably score that around 37. Rewrite it as: generated 1.8 million in qualified Salesforce pipeline. Now the search terms, metric, and result sit together, so 88 makes sense. Run the free Signal score before you apply.

Storyboard:
- Case: Account Executive Resume against Mid-Market Account Executive.
- Open on a reviewer search box over the resume, searching for Salesforce.
- Show the weak bullet that does not include the searched language.
- Search pipeline generation and show the miss again.
- Show the Signal Fit Score rubric before the score reveal.
- Rewrite the bullet and rerun the search with a visible match.
- Close after the low-score rationale explains the jump.
