## Creative Director Notes From Reviewing Winners

1. The artifact appears early.
   The highest-fit examples show a resume, resume section, job search artifact, or evidence object in the thumbnail or first beat. Signal must never open with a generic title card unless it is a cold headline over the resume itself.

2. The human is reacting, not narrating a commercial.
   Winning resume content sounds like "I noticed this" and "I would fix this", not "our tool analyzes your resume." The script should include natural reviewer moves: pause, read the line, react, explain, rewrite.

3. The promise is narrow.
   "One mistake", "one formula", "one experiment", and "one exact line" are clearer than broad "optimize your resume" promises.

4. The conflict is visible.
   Strong formats show an obvious problem: red box, misspelled section, search term not found, bad bullet, hidden hack, or recruiter eye-tracker result. Our score only works after the viewer has seen that conflict.

5. The best hooks carry tension.
   Useful hooks: "This looks fine, but I would not search it", "I hit Ctrl+F and got nothing", "This is the bullet I would rewrite first", "Do not use the viral resume hack", "This resume has the proof in the wrong place."

6. Shorts need less script than we thought.
   The short-form benchmark set proves 45-75 second clips can work, but Signal's generated shorts should be 18-32 seconds until we solve flow. One weak line is enough.

7. Score needs a receipt.
   Do not show "34 -> 92" unless the video first shows why: search terms missing, no metric, no tool, no outcome, or proof buried elsewhere. The score should feel like an afterthought to the human review, not the premise.

## Required New Pipeline Rules

### Research Gate

Every generated packet must include at least two benchmark references from this file:

- `trendResearch.benchmarkUrls`
- `trendResearch.borrowedMechanic`
- `trendResearch.whyThisMechanicFits`
- `trendResearch.whatNotToCopy`

Reject packets that only say "viral resume teardown" without naming the specific mechanic.

### Script Gate

The script must read like a cold human review:

- A reviewer physically or digitally opens a resume.
- The reviewer reads one weak line aloud.
- The reviewer reacts in plain human language.
- The reviewer points to one job requirement.
- The reviewer shows a visible reason the line fails.
- The reviewer finds real proof already on the resume.
- The reviewer rewrites the line using only that proof.
- The reviewer explains the score change with a receipt.
- Voiceover target is 38-72 words for 18-28 second clips; scripts longer than that tend to sound like explainers.

Ban abstract narration like:

- "This resume is invisible."
- "The rubric says..."
- "Signal optimizes..."
- "Same person, better signal."
- "I would score it around..."
- Any score reveal that is not tied to a visible receipt.

### Art Direction Gate

The video must pick one benchmark-inspired format:

1. Expert red-pen teardown: based on Business Insider's physical resume review.
2. Expressive face plus resume: based on Jeff Su's thumbnail style, but without copying his layout.
3. Recruiter experiment/search test: based on Jerry Lee's eye-tracker/search-test framing.
4. Taboo hack debunk: based on viral white-fonting discourse.
5. Job-seeker frustration setup: based on viral job-search confessionals, but with humor and a fix.

### Voice Gate

The voice must sound like someone reviewing the document in real time:

- Use first-person fragments.
- Add short pauses after reading the weak line.
- Allow one small human aside.
- Avoid perfect corporate cadence.
- Prefer speech-to-speech from a rough human scratch read over pure TTS.

## Immediate Direction For Signal

The next rebuilt short should not be another polished UI animation. It should be:

**Opening frame:** a realistic one-page resume on a desk or recruiter screen.

**First words:** "I would rewrite this line first."

**Visible problem:** weak bullet highlighted.

**Human reaction:** "It says you helped. That makes me guess."

**Job requirement:** one role requirement highlighted.

**Proof:** a real metric or tool already elsewhere on the resume.

**Rewrite:** one stronger line.

**Score receipt:** four tiny checks: keyword, metric, tool, outcome.

**CTA:** "Run the free Signal score before you apply."
