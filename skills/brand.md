# Signal Brand Guide

## Positioning

Signal helps job seekers make their real experience easier for recruiters to find. It compares a resume to a target job description, spots missing role language, and helps rewrite honest bullets without inventing facts.

## Offer

- Free Signal score before applying.
- One-time resume fix at $9.99.
- Resume and cover letter package can be promoted after the free-score value is clear.
- No subscription framing is important.

## Link

Use the current landing page with UTMs in video metadata and social bios. Do not put the raw URL on-screen unless Andrew explicitly asks for it.

```text
https://ats-hacker-swart.vercel.app/?utm_source=PLATFORM&utm_medium=short&utm_campaign=signal_growth
```

## Voice

Human reviewer, not AI SaaS narrator.

Good:

- "I'm stopping on this line."
- "The experience is probably fine. The sentence is useless."
- "The job post gave you the answer key."
- "Run the free Signal score before you apply."
- "Need yours fixed? Link in bio."

Avoid:

- "Unlock your career potential."
- "Beat the bots."
- "Guaranteed interviews."
- "ATS auto-rejected this resume."
- Repeating "Same person. Better signal."

## Visual Style

Use variety across batches:

- Recruiter search console.
- Red-marker resume roast.
- Job-description answer key/highlighter.
- Desk audit.
- Evidence board.
- Signal mascot rescue / filter breakthrough.

Do not let every short become the same dark neon SaaS ad. Signal can look premium, but the content should feel like a human is catching a real resume mistake.

Signal presence is optional:

- Resume-review and career-advice videos should usually show no mascot and no product UI.
- Signal appears as the fix in the spoken CTA, caption, post description, and bio link.
- Use the mascot only for clearly animated concept videos where Signal is the subject.

## Palette

- Deep navy/black background.
- Signal cyan/teal glow for product moments.
- Red for weak resume evidence.
- Green for verified fixes.
- White captions with dark outline for short-form readability.

## Trust Rules

- No fake experience.
- No fake outcomes.
- No invented customer stats.
- No claims that ATS automatically rejects resumes.
- Use the honest angle: ATS stores/indexes/ranks/searches; humans still review.
