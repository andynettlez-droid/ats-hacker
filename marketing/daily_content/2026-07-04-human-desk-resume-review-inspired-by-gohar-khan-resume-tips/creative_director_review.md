## What Changed

- Replaced the dashboard/SaaS-demo look with `ResumeDeskReview`: top-down desk, paper resume, nearby job post, marker motion, sticky-note score rationale.
- Rewrote scripts to sound like a human reviewer: "I am reading...", "This line says...", "I would write...".
- Removed forced meme slang and old repeated phrases from the generator and creative gate.
- Disabled implicit role inference so daily batches rotate real cases instead of repeating the same data/HubSpot-style example.
- Kept each short under 31 seconds with one idea, one weak line, one rewrite, and one quick free-score CTA.

## Compared With Reference

The reference wins through physical POV simplicity: one paper, one mistake, plain narration, no software framing. This batch now copies the useful structure without copying the asset: physical artifact first, human review voice, one visible mistake, quick payoff.

What still trails the reference:

- No real hands/camera footage. The synthetic desk scene is better than the old UI, but real B-roll would lift trust.
- Voice is better paced, but the configured ElevenLabs voice still sounds like generated narration in places. Owner voice clone or a more conversational creator voice should be the next audio test.
- Captions are readable but heavy. A future variant should test smaller top captions like the reference.

## Verdict

This batch is suitable for Codex review. It is a real improvement over the rejected AI-slop batch, but not yet the final creative ceiling. Do not auto-publish without human approval of the exact video and voice feel.
