# Longform Voiceover

## Cold open

This resume looks professional, and that is the trap. The job description screams HubSpot, CAC, LinkedIn Ads, and lifecycle marketing. The resume whispers helped with campaigns. That is invisible experience.

Then I will run the resume through a simple search test. Then we will find the mistake that makes a qualified person look generic. ## Translate the job description

Start with the job description, not the resume. A Demand Generation Manager posting is usually not asking for vague marketing energy. It is asking for pipeline sourced, HubSpot workflows, LinkedIn Ads, CAC analysis, lifecycle marketing, and MQL-to-SQL conversion. Those are not magic keywords. They are clues about what the hiring team wants to see quickly. If your resume has the experience but calls everything campaigns, support, and coordination, you are making the reviewer translate your background for you. Reviewers are not mind readers, and they are usually moving fast.

## Recruiter search test

Now pretend I am searching resumes. I type HubSpot. I type CAC. I type LinkedIn Ads. I type pipeline. If this candidate has done that work, the resume should give me a clean match. Right now, it barely does. That does not mean the person is bad. It means the resume is asking me to infer too much. This is where a lot of job seekers lose attention. They think the resume is being humble and professional. To a recruiter, it can read like polite fog.

## Resume crime scene

Here is the resume crime scene: helped maintain HubSpot lists for webinars and nurture emails. That is not bad experience. It is just under-labeled experience. The bullet has HubSpot, which is useful, but it hides scope, audience, and business result. What kind of lists? For what campaigns? What happened because of the work? The evidence is wearing camouflage. A stronger resume does not need to brag. It needs to remove the camouflage so the proof is visible in a six-second skim.

## Why it gets missed

This is not about tricking software. The ATS is not a wizard sitting in a basement rejecting good people for sport. The practical problem is simpler: resumes get stored, searched, skimmed, compared, and forgotten. When the job description says CAC analysis and your resume says helped with campaigns, the strongest version of your experience is not on the page yet. It is stuck in your memory. A hiring team should not have to interview you just to discover the basic proof that belonged in the bullet.

## Live bullet fix

Now we translate, not fabricate. The weak bullet says: helped maintain HubSpot lists for webinars and nurture emails. A stronger version says: built HubSpot lead-scoring and LinkedIn retargeting segments that cut CAC 32% and lifted MQL-to-SQL conversion 18%. Same work category. Same candidate. Much clearer proof. Notice what changed. We added the tool, the channel, the business metric, and the result. We did not add a fake job, a fake degree, or a fake skill. That is the line Signal has to protect.
